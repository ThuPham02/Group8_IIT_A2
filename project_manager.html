IT Project Manager

Image 1: Kanika Tolver - IT Project Manager

What kind of work is done by the IT professional?

They are the team's psychiatrist, they are accountable for setting the timetable, task, assisting the team in reaching the finish line, and removing risks, challenges, and obstructions. Assembling the team with the appropriate budget, resources, and technology in order to develop the project's strategy

What kinds of people does the IT professional interact with? Are they other IT professionals? Clients? Investors? The general public?

Meetings with management, deliberating over project-related issues, and team meetings with developers and testers are all part of her typical day. Additionally, weekly meetings are held to discuss finances and scheduling and she must solicit feedback directly from stakeholders.

Where do the IT professionals spend most of their time?

They gather needs from end-users and stakeholders every day. Then collaborate with developers. Many of these are daily standup meetings of 15 minutes to review the previous day's work and the day's agenda.

What aspect of their position is most challenging?

Kanika believes that insisting on transparency is critical while dealing with multiple parties. She said "I'm accountable for ensuring that my engineers understand end-user requirements and for notifying management to potentially dangerous situations or schedule changes. Maintaining an eye on the deadline and notifying everyone if we fall short." COVID-19 meetings were held online, making efficient use of internet tools even more difficult. At times, this entails "working a little bit harder, longer, and responding to emails more rapidly."
